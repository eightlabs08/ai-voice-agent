# Bright Smile Dental - Knowledge Base

## Insurance and Payment

**Q: What insurance plans do you accept?**
We accept most major dental insurance plans including Delta Dental, Cigna, Aetna, MetLife, Guardian, United Healthcare Dental, Blue Cross Blue Shield, and Humana. We also accept most PPO and HMO plans. If you are unsure whether we accept your plan, please call us and we will verify your coverage before your appointment.

**Q: What payment options are available?**
We accept cash, all major credit cards (Visa, Mastercard, American Express, Discover), personal checks, and HSA/FSA cards. We also offer CareCredit financing for procedures over $500, allowing you to pay in monthly installments with low or no interest. Payment is expected at the time of service.

**Q: Do you offer payment plans?**
Yes, we offer payment plans through CareCredit for procedures over $500. CareCredit provides 6-month and 12-month interest-free financing options. For larger treatments, extended plans up to 60 months are available with low interest rates. Ask our front desk for an application.

**Q: What if I do not have insurance?**
We welcome uninsured patients. We offer a Bright Smile Savings Plan for $199 per year that includes two cleanings, two exams, and all necessary X-rays, plus 15% off any additional treatments. This plan helps make dental care affordable without insurance.

## Services and Pricing

**Q: How much does a dental cleaning cost?**
A standard adult cleaning (prophylaxis) ranges from $100 to $200 depending on your insurance coverage. With most insurance plans, preventive cleanings are covered at 80-100%. A deep cleaning (scaling and root planing) for gum disease ranges from $200 to $400 per quadrant. We can provide a specific estimate after reviewing your insurance benefits.

**Q: How long does a dental cleaning take?**
A standard cleaning appointment is 30 to 45 minutes. If you are a new patient, please allow 60 minutes for your first visit as we will also take X-rays and perform a comprehensive exam. Deep cleaning appointments are typically 60 to 90 minutes.

**Q: What whitening services do you offer?**
We offer professional in-office whitening using the Zoom whitening system for $450, which takes about 90 minutes and can lighten teeth up to 8 shades. We also offer custom take-home whitening trays for $250, which include custom-fitted trays and professional-grade whitening gel for gradual results over 2 weeks.

**Q: What types of fillings do you offer?**
We offer tooth-colored composite fillings that match your natural tooth color. Prices range from $150 to $400 per filling depending on size and location. Most insurance plans cover fillings at 50-80%. We no longer place amalgam (silver) fillings.

**Q: Do you offer dental crowns?**
Yes, we offer porcelain and zirconia crowns. Prices range from $800 to $1,500 per crown depending on the material and complexity. Most insurance plans cover crowns at 50%. We can often complete same-day crowns using our CEREC technology.

**Q: Do you see children?**
Yes, we welcome patients of all ages, including children. We recommend a child's first dental visit by age one or within six months of their first tooth erupting. Our team is experienced with pediatric patients and we strive to make dental visits fun and stress-free for kids.

## Appointments and Policies

**Q: What are your office hours?**
We are open Monday through Friday from 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM and Saturday from 9:00 AM to 1:00 PM. We are closed on Sundays. The last appointment slot is 30 minutes before closing.

**Q: What is your cancellation policy?**
We require at least 24 hours notice for cancellations or rescheduling. Late cancellations or no-shows may incur a $50 fee. We understand emergencies happen, so please call us as soon as possible if you need to change your appointment. We will always work with you.

**Q: How early should I arrive for my appointment?**
Please arrive 10 to 15 minutes before your scheduled appointment time. This allows time for check-in and any paperwork. New patients should arrive 15 minutes early to complete patient registration forms. You can also fill out forms online before your visit at our website.

**Q: What should new patients bring to their first visit?**
New patients should bring a valid photo ID, their insurance card, a list of current medications (including supplements), and any dental records or X-rays from their previous dentist if available. If you have dental anxiety, please let us know ahead of time so we can accommodate you.

## Emergency Care

**Q: What do I do in a dental emergency?**
For life-threatening emergencies (difficulty breathing, uncontrolled bleeding, jaw fracture), call 911 immediately. For urgent dental issues during office hours, call us at (555) 234-5678 and we will fit you in the same day whenever possible. After hours, our answering service can connect you with our on-call dentist for guidance.

**Q: What counts as a dental emergency?**
Dental emergencies include severe toothache, knocked-out or broken teeth, lost fillings or crowns causing pain, dental abscesses (swelling with fever), and injuries to the mouth or jaw. If you are unsure, call us and we will help determine if you need immediate care.
